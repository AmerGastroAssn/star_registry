Welcome to the first AGA STAR Registry Newsletter! The STAR Registry team is thankful for your support of this important initiative and hopes to keep in touch with you via these newsletters, which will provide you with insight from other sites, answers to frequently asked questions, links to resources, enrollment metrics, and more.

AGA is looking to the future and wants to ensure that GIs have innovative tools to care for their patients. With your support, we are collecting critical data to support this process and bring new technology to practice in a safe and timely manner.

Here are the goals of AGA’s Registry initiative:

  1. As a neutral, objective broker, AGA will establish observational research registries to help companies gather the data needed by payors, purchasers, risk-bearing organization and regulatory agencies to support the approval, coverage, reimbursement and adoption of new technologies, therapies and procedures.
  2. Develop observational research registries to help bring new medical devices to gastroenterologists and their patients.
  3. Provide data to allow stakeholders to evaluate the efficacy of a device and document safety and durability.

A Message from Ashish Atreja, MD, MPH, chair of the AGA Registry Oversight Subcommittee
“The announcement of the AGA STAR Registry in March 2014 was a huge milestone for the AGA Center for GI Innovation and Technology. With this registry, we’re setting new standards for comparative evaluation of new technologies in the GI space. The STAR Registry will be the first registry to compare Laparoscopic Nissen Fundoplication (LNF) surgery versus Transoral Incisionless Fundoplication (TIF®). The goal is to follow 500 patients over three years to gather a critical source of long-term data to compare the safety, effectiveness and side-effect profile of these two procedures. This real-world data will advance the treatment of GERD patients, and we are grateful for your participation in this important initiative.”

Hear more from Dr. Atreja in this video interview.
A Message from W. Scott Melvin, MD, principal investigator from Ohio State University
“Surgeons are actively involved in the care of the patient with troublesome reflux. While traditional surgical approaches can be effective, less invasive incisionless approaches should be considered in the correct patient population. New data collection techniques and groups with high-quality data can help guide us as clinicians in guiding our patients to provide the best possible care options.”
A Message from Brian M. Fennerty, MD, principal investigator from Oregon Health and Science University
“As a gastroenterologist and a principal investigator of the STAR Registry, I need to know the effect and durability that TIF has in order to inform my patients of their options for managing their GERD. This registry will provide me with that information and thus the opportunity to better inform patients of their options other than LNF or medical therapy for this disease. This registry represents a unique opportunity for optimizing patient care and improving treatment of one of the most common digestive disorders. I appreciate your participation in this important research endeavor.”
FAQs
We have received several questions regarding the use of VieDocs, our Electronic Case Report File (eCRF).
 
What eCRF is the STAR registry using?
The STAR registry is utilizing VieDocs, which is an electronic data capture system chosen for its simplistic and modern designed eCRFs.
 
How do I gain access to VieDocs?
You will receive an email from our data manager Vijay Moolaveesala (Vmoolaveesala@aciclinical.com), which will provide instructions for the training process, how to verify training was completed and what communication to expect from VieDocs.
 
I have completed the training but I still do not have access. What do I do?
Once the site staff has completed the VieDocs training it is important to complete and return the system user form before staff can be given access to the eCRFs.
